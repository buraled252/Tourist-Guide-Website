Rogers Centre

Rogers Centre (originally SkyDome) is a multi-purpose retractable roof stadium in Downtown Toronto, Ontario, Canada, situated at the base of the CN Tower near the northern shore of Lake Ontario. Opened in 1989 on the former Railway Lands, it is home to the Toronto Blue Jays of Major League Baseball (MLB). Previously, the stadium was also home to the Toronto Argonauts of the Canadian Football League (CFL) and the Toronto Raptors of the National Basketball Association (NBA). The Buffalo Bills of the National Football League (NFL) played an annual game at the stadium as part of the Bills Toronto Series from 2008 to 2013. While it is primarily a sports venue, it also hosts other large events such as conventions, trade fairs, concerts, travelling carnivals, circuses and monster truck shows. More Information

CN Tower

The CN Tower is a beloved Toronto landmark that stands tall at an impressive 553 meters above the city. As one of the most recognizable structures in Canada, the tower attracts visitors from all over the world. Completed in 176, the CN Tower was originally built as a communications tower for the Canadian National Railway. Today it has become a symbol of Toronto's modernity and innovation. Visitors can take a ride on the glass elevator to the top of the tower, where they'll find an observation deck that offers breathtaking panoramic views of Toronto and the surrounding area. For those looking for a thrilling adventure, the tower also offers the Edge Walk experience. This oneofa kind activity allows visitors to walk along a five foot wide ledge that encircles the tower's main pod. More Information

Royal Ontario Museum

The Royal Ontario Museum is a true gem of Toronto, offering visitors an extensive collection of art, culture and natural history. Founded in 1912, the museum has grown to become one of the largest in North America. With over 6 million objects in its collection, its exhibits are both educational and entertaining. The natural history exhibits are particularly popular with the museum, boasting an extensive collection of dinosaur fossils, including one of the largest and most complete Tyrannosaurus Rex skeletons in the world.
